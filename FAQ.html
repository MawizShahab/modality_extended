What is a mental health paraprofessional?

A mental health paraprofessionals is a helper who extends the reach of mental health services, helping to bring mental health care to places where ther aren't enough therapists or doctors.

While paraprofessionals don't have a full professional license, they know how to do many effective things for people who need subclinical care. They watch how people act, help them with everyday struggles, connect people to resources, and give kind support.

Paraprofessionals work in different places, like big hospitals for mental health, vitually, in homes where people get help, and places in the community that help with mental health

This job is also a good career path for people who like to help others and are interested in the field of mental health, but who may not have the desire or resources to pursue full licensure.

How does Modality account for clinical safety and legal risk?

+

Our platform is developed in consultation with expert lawyers and clinician researchers around evidence-based practices, regulatory compliance, professional training, consent and education, monitoring and reporting, ethical considerations, and privacy. At certain subscription tiers, Modality members have access to on-demand questions and answers about legal liability and clinical consultation.
